## Module Overview
The General Ledger (GL) is the central repository for all financial transactions recorded across the ERP. In Philippine accounting, the GL must strictly comply with the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) requirements for Computerized Accounting Systems (CAS). This module acts as a read-only consolidation of all posted entries (from Journal Entries, Vouchers, Invoices, Receipts, etc.), serving as the single source of truth for generating the Trial Balance and Financial Statements. It ensures absolute data integrity; records here are immutable and trace back directly to their source documents.

## Dashboard UI
The General Ledger interface is designed as an analytical and reporting tool, rather than a data entry screen.

### The Action Bar
* **Search Bar:** Search by Document Number, Reference, or Description.
* **Filter:** Comprehensive filters for Date Range (Start Date to End Date), Account Range (e.g., 1000 to 1999), and Source Module (e.g., AP, AR, JE).
* **Customize View:** Adjust visible columns.
* **Export Button:** Export the GL to Excel or CSV, crucial for BIR audit submissions and external auditor requests.
* **Print Button:** Generate a formal GL report.
* **Import Button:** Not applicable (The GL is system-generated and read-only).

### The Data List
The GL presents a chronological log of all debits and credits.
* **Posting Date:** The accounting date of the transaction.
* **Source Document:** E.g., JE-2026-0001, INV-2026-105. Hyperlinked to the original document.
* **Account Code & Name:** The specific GL account affected.
* **Reference:** External reference number.
* **Description:** Line-level explanation.
* **Debit:** Amount debited.
* **Credit:** Amount credited.
* **Running Balance:** Calculated balance for the specific account.

## Data Fields

The General Ledger does not have an entry form. It consists of immutable records generated automatically upon the posting of financial documents from other modules.

### Line Item Data (System Generated)
**CRITICAL VALIDATION:** Total Debits across the entire GL must always equal Total Credits. The GL posting behavior guarantees that no unbalanced entry can ever hit these tables. Modifying GL records directly is strictly prohibited to maintain BIR CAS compliance. Reversals must be done via the originating module.
