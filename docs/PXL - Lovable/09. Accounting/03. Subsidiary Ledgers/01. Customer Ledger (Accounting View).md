# PXL ERP Blueprint: Customer Ledger (Accounting View)

## Module Overview
The Customer Ledger (Accounts Receivable Subsidiary Ledger) provides a detailed, customer-by-customer breakdown of the Accounts Receivable (AR) balance. In Philippine enterprise accounting, while sales and collections teams look at invoices, the accounting department needs this ledger to reconcile the total AR per customer against the singular AR Control Account in the General Ledger. It strictly tracks invoice issuances (debits) and collection receipts/credit memos (credits) for a specific customer.

## Dashboard UI
This interface is a specialized analytical view for AR accountants and credit controllers.

### The Action Bar
* **Customer Selector:** A searchable dropdown to select the specific Customer.
* **Date Range Filter:** "From" and "To" date pickers to isolate the period.
* **Export Button:** Exports the customer ledger to Excel/CSV for sending Statements of Account (SOA) or for internal reconciliation.
* **Print Button:** Generates a PDF SOA.
* **View Unapplied Payments:** A quick filter toggle to show only overpayments or advances that haven't been applied to invoices.

### The Data List
The screen displays a summary header and transaction lines.
**Header Summary:**
* Customer Name and TIN
* Opening Balance (as of Start Date)
* Total Billed (Debits)
* Total Paid/Credited (Credits)
* Current Outstanding Balance

**Transaction Lines:**
* **Date:** Posting date of the transaction.
* **Document Type:** E.g., Sales Invoice, Official Receipt, Credit Memo.
* **Document Number:** Hyperlinked to the source.
* **Description:** Transaction memo.
* **Debit (Billed):** Invoice amounts increasing AR.
* **Credit (Paid):** Payment or credit memo amounts decreasing AR.
* **Running Balance:** The progressive outstanding balance.

## Supabase Database Architecture

The Customer Ledger leverages the unified `general_ledger_entries` table, specifically utilizing the `contact_id` column.

### Data Flow & Logic
1.  **Filtering by Contact:** The core query filters `general_ledger_entries` where `contact_id = [Selected Customer UUID]` AND the `account_id` belongs to the Accounts Receivable account group.
2.  **Aggregation:** It calculates the opening balance based on activity prior to the start date, and then chronologically lists transactions within the date range.

*Note: In robust ERPs, a separate AR subledger table might exist for extreme performance, but for this architecture, querying the GL with a `contact_id` index is highly efficient and guarantees 100% reconciliation with the Trial Balance.*

```sql
-- Index required for fast subledger querying
CREATE INDEX idx_gle_contact_account ON general_ledger_entries (contact_id, account_id, posting_date);
```
