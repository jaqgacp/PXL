# PXL ERP Blueprint: Supplier Ledger (Accounting View)

## Module Overview
The Supplier Ledger (Accounts Payable Subsidiary Ledger) is the mirror image of the Customer Ledger. It provides a detailed, supplier-by-supplier breakdown of the Accounts Payable (AP) balance. For Philippine accounting teams, this is critical for managing cash flow, prioritizing vendor disbursements, and reconciling the total outstanding payables against the AP Control Account in the General Ledger. It tracks bills/vouchers (credits to AP) and disbursement payments/debit memos (debits to AP).

## Dashboard UI
A specialized analytical view for AP accountants and treasury personnel.

### The Action Bar
* **Supplier Selector:** A searchable dropdown to select the specific Supplier/Vendor.
* **Date Range Filter:** "From" and "To" date pickers to isolate the period.
* **Export Button:** Exports to Excel/CSV for vendor reconciliation or cash flow planning.
* **Print Button:** Generates a PDF ledger statement.
* **View Unapplied Advances:** Toggle to show prepayments made to the supplier not yet applied to a bill.

### The Data List
**Header Summary:**
* Supplier Name and TIN
* Opening Balance (as of Start Date)
* Total Billed (Credits)
* Total Paid/Debited (Debits)
* Current Outstanding Balance

**Transaction Lines:**
* **Date:** Posting date.
* **Document Type:** E.g., Bill, Voucher, Check Disbursement, Debit Memo.
* **Document Number:** Hyperlinked to the source.
* **Description:** Transaction memo.
* **Debit (Paid):** Payments decreasing AP.
* **Credit (Billed):** Bills increasing AP.
* **Running Balance:** The progressive outstanding payable balance.

## Supabase Database Architecture

Similar to the Customer Ledger, this relies on the `general_ledger_entries` table.

### Data Flow & Logic
1.  **Filtering by Contact:** Queries `general_ledger_entries` where `contact_id = [Selected Supplier UUID]` AND the `account_id` belongs to the Accounts Payable account group.
2.  **Aggregation:** Calculates the opening balance and chronological transactions. Note that for AP, credits increase the balance and debits decrease it.
